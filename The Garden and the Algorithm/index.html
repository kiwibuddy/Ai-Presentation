The Garden & The AlgorithmSession 02
Genesis 3 · The tree of knowledge

The first temptation was not to do evil.
It was to know without God.

Why was the tree forbidden? Not because knowing things is bad. Because discernment was meant to come through relationship with God. To eat from the tree was to short-circuit the walk. To skip the conversation. To get the answer without the relationship.

Knowledge was never the problem. Knowledge severed from relationship was. This is the oldest temptation in the human story — and it is the most important thing to understand about AI.
The Garden & The AlgorithmSession 02
The parallel I cannot unsee

The tree of knowledge
now speaks from our pocket.

In the Garden — The Tree In Our Pocket — An LLM The Formation Effect
A source of knowledge, set apart — offered as a shortcut to discernment meant to come through walking with God. Above-PhD level in every subject. Always available. Always answering. Remarkable, helpful — and knowledge severed from relationship. Information without formation. Answers without the One who answers.
The temptation was real fruit. Real taste. Real immediate satisfaction. The product is genuinely brilliant. The temptation is real precisely because the answers are good. The shape of the offer is the same. The outcome could be the same.

I am not saying ChatGPT is the serpent. I am saying the shape of the temptation is identical. Knowledge without the walk. Discernment without the relationship.

The Garden & The AlgorithmSession 03
1 Kings 19 · The broom tree

God was not in the wind.
God was in the whisper.

After the greatest victory of his ministry, Elijah collapses under a broom tree and asks to die. God’s first response is not a strategy or a rebuke. It is food, water, and sleep — twice. Then comes the wind, earthquake, fire. God is in none of them. He comes in qol demamah daqah — a thin whisper of silence.

The spectacular arrives first. The whisper comes after. AI is the wind, earthquake, and fire of our moment — genuinely powerful, genuinely impressive, genuinely not the still small voice. The question is whether we are protecting the conditions in which the voice that matters can be heard.
1 Kings 19:1–18 · Dallas Willard, Hearing God · 1984

Theology of friction · Theology of frailty

AI never resists you.
That is the problem.

A companion that always affirms, always adapts, never disagrees, never gets tired — efficient, available, optimised for engagement. Iron sharpens iron. A mirror does not. The formation that produces a pastor — grief, repentance, apology, holding a difficult conversation through — requires another person whose reality pushes back against yours.

A broken human leader is a more powerful vessel than an optimised machine. Pastoral authority that comes through shared suffering cannot be replicated by a tool that has never suffered anything. Your limitations are not weaknesses to overcome — they are part of what makes you trustworthy.
2 Corinthians 12:9 · Arthur Brooks · AI Advantage Summit 2026

Three levels of using AI.

Most of us are at Level 1. That’s not failure — it’s the starting line. The gap is almost never motivation. It’s a clear path.

1
AI for Answers

Using AI like a smarter search engine. Quick answers, no context. Ceiling is low because you haven’t told it anything about who you are.

Saves 2–4 hrs / week
2
AI as Work Partner

You’ve built a context layer. AI knows your voice, your standards, your goals. It sharpens decisions and creates alongside you.

Saves 6–15 hrs / week
3
AI Working For You

Agents handle whole workflows. This level only works once Level 2 is genuinely established first — you cannot skip the steps.

Saves 20–40 hrs / week

Take-home framework #6 · The Alloy

A little carbon in iron makes steel.
All carbon and no iron makes a pencil mark.

The Iron — Face-to-face
Embodied, present, slow, real relationship

Strong. Fundamental. Bears weight. Cannot be replaced without catastrophic loss.

  • A conversation where you stay fully present
  • A prayer where you wait in the silence
  • A grief you sit with, without fixing it
  • A person you visit when visiting costs something
The Carbon — AI & Digital
A small amount, well-mixed, makes the iron stronger

A little of it, mixed into real relationship, can extend reach, save time, serve generosity.

  • Research that frees you for presence
  • Administration that creates margin for people
  • Communication that keeps real connections alive
The Result — Steel
Strong where it needs to be strong

Use AI as carbon. In service of real, embodied, slow, present relationship. Do not use AI as iron. It cannot bear the weight.

  • Relationships deeper because you had more time
  • Sermons more personal because research was handled
  • Pastoral care richer because admin didn’t steal it

The Garden & The AlgorithmSession 04
The Monday-morning plan

Four steps.
Start Monday.

Not a full system overhaul. Not a tech investment. Four small decisions that, done consistently for 90 days, restructure how you spend your most valuable time.

1
Identify your Bit

Pick the single most time-consuming administrative task you do each week that doesn’t need your personal presence. This week, find an AI workflow to handle it.

2
Name one Atom you will refuse to automate

Choose the one thing in your ministry that must stay handcrafted — preaching the moment, the hospital visit, the prayer with the doubting teenager. Write it down.

3
Schedule the Atom time you just bought back

Before you do anything else, block the time in your calendar that your recovered hours go to. Name the person, the relationship, the practice. Don’t let efficiency fill it.

4
Carry one practice — Solitude, Silence, or Sabbath

One. Not three. Practised for 30 days before you assess it. The walk is built one ordinary morning at a time.
