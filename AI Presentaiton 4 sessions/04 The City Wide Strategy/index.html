Before the Commission — The Positive Case

The same tools that
concern us can serve
the mission.

📚
Education Access
Personalised tutoring at scale — reaching children in the Global South who have never had access to differentiated instruction. In Tanzania, one teacher with an AI tool now delivers what previously required a 1:1 ratio.
Khan Academy · World Bank · 2024-25
🌍
Translation and the Great Commission
~2,000 languages still lack a complete Bible translation. AI-assisted translation could compress timelines from decades to years. Wycliffe and SIL are already deploying this in partnership with local translators.
Wycliffe Bible Translators · 2025
📡
Access Equalisation
A pastor in rural Kenya with a smartphone now has research and teaching support that previously required a seminary library. AI is arriving in many majority-world contexts as democratiser, not disruptor.
GSMA · UNESCO · 2025
The same AI that raises the concerns in Part 1 and Part 2 is the AI that can accelerate the Great Commission at a scale the church has never had access to. The question is always: who is holding it, with what values, toward what end?

The New Competitive Advantage

In a world where AI
can replicate anything —
ethics becomes rare.

🤝
Trust
In an environment of deepfakes and AI-generated everything, demonstrable trustworthiness is a strategic asset. The organisation known to tell the truth is the organisation people turn to when they need to know what is actually true.
🪞
Presence
Physical presence is now the premium product. AI cannot show up. The leader who is consistently, reliably, visibly there — in the room, in the hard conversations, in the long seasons — has something that cannot be generated or scaled.
🏛️
Constancy
AI optimises for engagement. Engagement algorithms respond to novelty, outrage, and validation. The community that stays constant — same values, same people, same commitments — through technological upheaval becomes a refuge in the truest sense.
Ethics is no longer a compliance requirement. In 2026, it is a differentiation strategy. The church has been practising this for two thousand years. The technology just made it visible how rare it is.

Ephesians 5:16 — Making the Most of Every Opportunity

Redeem the time.
The days are evil.

Paul wrote those words into a specific cultural moment of crisis and opportunity. The church in Ephesus was navigating a city shaped by imperial power, religious plurality, and economic complexity. His instruction was not to retreat. It was to redeem — to buy back the time, to use the moment wisely, to act with urgency and clarity.

The theological frame for AI leadership is not fear, worship, or sleepwalking. It is redemption. The same time, the same moment, the same city — but used with wisdom toward the purposes of God. That is the calling.
“
Be very careful, then, how you live — not as unwise but as wise, making the most of every opportunity, because the days are evil.
Ephesians 5:15-16 — NIV

The Church as Shock Absorber

When disruption hits,
social capital determines
who survives well.

Communities with strong social capital — bonds of trust and obligation that exist independent of economic productivity — suffer less during major disruptions and recover faster. The church, at its best, is exactly that community: multigenerational, geographically local, built on covenant relationship rather than transactional value.

What the Economy Cannot Provide
Trans-economic Community
When your job disappears, your productivity metric vanishes, and your economic identity is disrupted — the church is the community where your worth was never measured that way to begin with.
Covenant > Contract
What the Church Is Uniquely Positioned to Offer
Multigenerational Mentorship
Older members who have navigated disruption before. Younger members who need anchoring. The same community holding both across generations — something no algorithm can replicate or substitute.
Present > Digital
The AI disruption is a pastoral event as much as an economic one. The community that shows up for people in their hardest season is not a nice add-on to the response strategy. It is the response strategy.

Your Next 90 Days

Conviction without
action is just knowledge.

Days 1–30
Assess
  • Complete the Sacred Tasks exercise for your role
  • Identify your current Level of AI engagement (1, 2, or 3)
  • Name the primary formation question in your sphere
  • Audit what you are already using AI for — and what it is costing
Days 31–60
Act
  • Implement one Context Sandwich workflow this week
  • Introduce one counter-formation practice into your community
  • Identify one 18-35 year old you will mentor through this transition
  • Protect one Sacred Task you were on the verge of automating
Days 61–90
Advocate
  • Share what you have learned with one other leader in your network
  • Advocate for an AI policy in your church or organisation
  • Identify one Activate use case that extends your mission capacity
  • Write down what faithfulness looks like for you specifically in this season

The Three-Part Charge
Do not
Fear.
Fear produces paralysis or avoidance — neither of which is faithful. The leaders who respond to AI from anxiety will be reactive, late, and shaped by whatever the loudest voice is saying. You have been given a spirit not of fear but of power, love, and self-control.
Nathaniel Baldock · 20262 Timothy 1:7
The Three-Part Charge
Do not
Worship.
Worship produces uncritical adoption — using the tools without asking the formation question, building on the sand of technological optimism. Keller said it clearly: a good thing turned into an absolute value is idolatry. The efficiency gains are real. They are not the point.
Nathaniel Baldock · 2026Timothy Keller, Counterfeit Gods (2009)
The Three-Part Charge
Do not
Sleepwalk.
Sleepwalking is the most common failure mode — continuing as if nothing has changed, hoping it will sort itself out, delegating the question to someone else. The ground has shifted. You have been shown why. The leaders who act with clarity now will shape what this city looks like in 2031.
