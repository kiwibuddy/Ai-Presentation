The First Distinction — Dr. Arthur Brooks, Harvard

AI solves complicated.
Leaders navigate complex.

Complicated
Has a Solution
Many moving parts, but the right expertise applied correctly produces the right answer. A car engine. A tax return. A marketing funnel. A scheduling system.

AI is extraordinary here. These tasks are being transferred to machines at high speed.
Complex
Has No Solution — Only Navigation
Involves people with agency, competing values, unpredictable responses. A marriage. A congregation. A grieving team. A community in crisis.

AI cannot navigate this. It requires formed humans. This is where leadership actually lives.
The more AI handles the complicated, the more your value as a leader is concentrated in the complex. This is not a threat to your role. It is a clarification of it — if you have done the formation work that Part 2 was about.

How to Engage — The Three Levels

Most leaders are at Level 1.
The mission needs Level 3 thinking.

1
AI as Tool
Using specific AI applications to do defined tasks better. Point solutions for defined problems. You remain in control of every input and output.
ChatGPT for a first draft. Otter.ai for meeting notes. Canva AI for a graphic.
2
AI as Collaborator
Working with AI as a thinking partner that augments your output across a project or decision. The judgment is yours — the research, synthesis and drafting are shared.
Working through a strategic decision with AI analysis. Refining a teaching series with AI as an editorial partner.
3
AI as System
Designing workflows and organisations where AI handles defined streams autonomously, freeing people for the complex and sacred work that requires human presence.
An automated pastoral care follow-up pipeline. A self-updating communications calendar. An AI-driven content translation workflow.
Leaders who only operate at Level 1 will find themselves doing manual work alongside AI-native organisations. Leaders who jump to Level 3 without Level 2 wisdom will lose the formation awareness that makes Level 3 safe. The path is sequential.

The Four Levers

Not every task should
be automated. Know which
lever you are pulling.

🤖
Automate
Hand off the task entirely. AI runs it without your involvement. For tasks where the outcome is defined, repeatable, and the struggle is not formative.
🔍
Augment
Use AI to enhance your own thinking and output. You are still doing the work — better. The judgment, presence and relationships remain yours.
⚡
Accelerate
Use AI to speed up existing processes without changing their fundamental nature. Faster research, faster synthesis, faster communication.
🚀
Activate
Use AI to unlock work you could not previously do at all. New mission capacity. The small team doing the work of a large one. The church that could never afford a communications director — now running one.
Most leaders default to Automate when their mission is best served by Activate. The tractor did not automate the farmer out of farming. It activated a new scale of mission that would have been impossible without it.

Practical Skill — The Context Sandwich

Generic prompts get
generic answers.

The single most effective upgrade to your AI output requires no technical knowledge. It requires knowing your own context well enough to describe it clearly.

Top Layer — Context
Who you are. Your role, your community, your values, your organisation. The more specific, the better the output.
Middle — The Task
The actual question, request or task. Clear and specific.
Bottom Layer — Constraints
Format, length, tone, what to avoid. "No jargon. 250 words. Warm, not corporate."
Without top and bottom layers, AI defaults to generic responses calibrated for an average user. With them, it responds as a well-briefed assistant who actually knows your context. This is the whole game. Click for example →

The Bits vs Atoms Framework

AI lives in Bits.
Mission lives in Atoms.

Bits — Digital Domain
AI is extraordinary here
  • Content, data, information, code
  • Analysis and synthesis
  • Communication and translation
  • Research and pattern-finding
  • Anything that can be digitised
Atoms — Embodied Domain
AI cannot cross this line
  • Your physical presence in a room
  • Tears at a funeral — real ones
  • A hand on a shoulder at the right moment
  • The trust built over ten years of showing up
  • The incarnational, embodied core of mission
God chose atoms. The incarnation is the definitive statement that embodied presence matters. The Church's deepest calling is always an atoms calling. The strategic question: where are you accidentally treating atoms work like bits work — and losing what makes it powerful?

Exercise — What You Are Actually Protecting

Sacred Tasks.
What must not
be delegated.

The First Question
Does this task require a human being?
Some tasks genuinely need human judgment, empathy, or presence. Others simply have a human doing them out of habit.
If no → automate with confidence.
The Second Question
Does it require this specific human — you?
Your history, your relationships, your calling in this community. Some tasks could be done by anyone with the right skill — others require you specifically.
If no → delegate or systematise.
The Third Question
Is the struggle part of the formation?
Is doing this task — wrestling with it — forming you or the people you serve? The difficulty might be the point. Not all struggle should be removed.
If yes → protect it. This is Sacred.
Most leaders find roughly 20% of their tasks are genuinely Sacred — and that they spend far too much time on the other 80%. The exercise clarifies fast. And it removes the guilt from handing off everything else.

The Leadership Framework — Five Disciplines

Five disciplines for
leading through this.

1
Discernment
Ask the formation question before the efficiency question. What is this tool training the people in my sphere to desire? Is that what I want them to desire?
2
Dignity
Treat every person in your sphere as an image-bearer, not an optimisation target. Their worth does not depend on what AI can or cannot do better than them.
3
Design
Build systems and workflows that use AI without deforming the people inside them. Protect the formation pipeline. Know which tasks are Sacred before you automate them.
4
Discipleship
Actively form the next generation of leaders in the habits AI cannot replicate: truthfulness, presence, embodied love, the ability to sit with the unresolvable.
5
Mission
Use AI to extend the reach of the mission — translation, content, admin, research — without replacing the human core of it. Activate, don't amputate.
Five disciplines, one posture. Hold these together and AI becomes a servant of the mission. Drop any one and the others bend out of shape — efficiency without dignity, design without discipleship, mission without discernment.

Counter-Formation Practices

The disciplines that
form what AI cannot.

🌿
Exodus 20:8-11 — Sabbath
Sabbath as Resistance
A weekly declaration that the world does not require your constant productivity to hold together. In an always-on culture, Sabbath is the most countercultural thing you can do. It trains the posture of dependence that AI culture most wants to dissolve.
🤝
John 1:14 — Incarnation
Embodiment as Theology
God became flesh. Physical presence matters. In a world of infinite disembodied interaction, showing up in person — sharing a meal, sitting in silence — is not pastoral niceness. It is a theological act.
🪞
Proverbs 27:17 — Honest witness
Truthfulness as Currency
Sycophantic AI tells people what they want to hear. The counter-formation practice is the commitment to say the thing that costs something, to name what is real. In a world of AI flattery, genuine honesty is the rarest currency.
🧑‍🏫
Matthew 28:19 — Make disciples
Apprenticeship as Mission
Protect the formation pipeline. Identify which entry-level tasks are formative rather than merely productive. Deliberately keep them in human hands. The Great Commission is the original counter-formation strategy.

The Seven Spheres — David Hamilton

AI enters every sphere
simultaneously.

The Seven Spheres of Society framework prevents AI from being evaluated only in economic terms. Each sphere has its own formation question. Each sphere is being entered right now.

Family
AI companions targeting the intimacy hunger of isolated people.
Economics
AI displacing entry-level workers and concentrating economic power.
Government
AI surveillance, deepfakes, political manipulation at scale.
Religion
1 in 3 adults trust AI spiritual advice as much as a pastor.
Education
AI tutors available to every child — and forming every child.
Media
AI generating the majority of online content — truth and lies at equal scale.
Arts
AI-generated creativity democratised and devalued simultaneously.
Each sphere asks the same formation question: what is AI training the people in this sphere to desire — and is that what God intends for them?
