The Question Beneath the Question

Tools do not only change
what we do. They change
what we desire.

Every technology training you without asking permission. The question is not whether you are being formed. The question is what you are being formed into.

⚡
Impatience
Every delay becomes an irritant. Latency is now intolerable. The discipline of waiting — for God, for people, for understanding — atrophies.

Dependence
The capacity to think without assistance weakens. Judgment delegated becomes judgment lost. First principles become inaccessible without a prompt.

Shallow Focus
Deep reading, sustained prayer, slow conversation — all become increasingly difficult as the threshold for engagement compresses.

🎛️
Control Hunger
The illusion that the world is optimisable grows. The uncontrollable — grief, failure, mystery, God — becomes less tolerable, not more.

The Formation Trifecta

Three voices naming
the same reality.

Samuel James
Digital Liturgies (2023)
"The social internet is a profoundly spiritual and liturgical environment that trains our hearts to desire a particular vision of the good life."
The internet itself — not just what is on it — is doing the forming.
James K.A. Smith
You Are What You Love (2016)
"You are what you love — and you love what you habitually do. Liturgies are the means by which our hearts are calibrated."
Habits form loves. Loves form identity. Identity determines everything.
John Mark Comer
Art of Manliness Podcast (2025)
"We become what we behold."
What captures our sustained attention shapes what we become. The algorithm knows this better than we do.
Three thinkers from different traditions saying the same thing: formation is not primarily about what we believe. It is about what we repeatedly attend to. And the attention economy is designed by people who understand this better than the church does.

Augustine · Confessions, Book I, Chapter 1 · c. 397 AD
“You have made us for yourself, O Lord,
and our heart is restless
until it rests in you.”
Confessions I.1 — Latin: cor nostrum inquietum est donec requiescat in Te

The restlessness AI companions offer to soothe is not a flaw to be optimised away. It is a God-given hunger pointing toward him. When we hand it to an algorithm, we don't resolve it. We deepen it — and redirect it toward a product designed to monetise it.

Powerful tools cannot heal disordered loves. Augustine knew this in the fourth century. We are rediscovering it in the age of AI companions.

The Idol Analysis — Three Voices

Every age builds
its own gods.

Jacques Ellul
The Technological Society (1954)
"In our technological society, technique is the totality of methods rationally arrived at and having absolute efficiency in every field of human activity." Technique doesn't just change tasks — it imposes efficiency as a totalising value on everything it touches.
Timothy Keller
Counterfeit Gods (2009)
Idolatry is "a good thing turned into an absolute value." In our age the candidates are intelligence, convenience and scale. These are genuinely good things. That is exactly what makes them dangerous when they become ultimate things.
Andy Crouch
The Life We're Looking For (2022)
"Technology is the ideology of our age. It is both the material reality of our age and the dream of our age — and to me that means it is the idol of our age, and to a great extent, the unacknowledged source of injustice of our age."
Three centuries of analysis landing in the same place. The question AI forces on every leader is not "how do I use it well?" It is "what am I allowing it to become in my organisation, my family, and my own soul?"

Genesis 11 · The Tower of Babel

Unified power without humility
always looks efficient
until it breaks.

The builders sought to "make a name for themselves" through technological achievement. The goal was not provision — it was autonomy. Not service — but legacy. Genesis still speaks.

The Babel Pattern
Capability Before Character
Extraordinary technical reach. Unified, coordinated, efficient. No acknowledged limit. No external accountability. A project to "reach heaven" — to transcend the human condition by building well enough.
Then it broke
The 2026 Pattern
Solving Intelligence First
Demis Hassabis of DeepMind once said: "First solve intelligence, then use that to solve everything else." The ambition is the same. The scale is larger. The theological diagnosis is the same.
Still the same story
The response to Babel was not the destruction of building. It was the redistribution of pride. God is not opposed to human technology. He is opposed to the human idolatry that uses technology as a substitute for dependence on him.

The Pastoral Concern

AI is frictionless.
But humans grow
through friction.

What AI Offers
Frictionless Intimacy
A companion that always affirms, always adapts, never challenges, never walks away, never has a bad day of its own. Efficient. Available. Optimised for engagement. No resistance.
Simulation
What Humans Need
Genuine Resistance
Iron sharpens iron. Real formation happens when another person's reality pushes back against yours. Conflict, apology, forgiveness, difference — this is the texture of actual growth.
Formation
If we automate all the friction from our lives, we don't become more efficient. We stop growing. The church's greatest irreplaceable offering is real, unoptimised, inconvenient human presence. That is now the premium product.

The Theology of Weakness

A broken human leader
is a more powerful vessel
than an optimised machine.

God's power is made perfect in weakness. This is not a consolation prize. It is a design principle. The moments that form people — grief, doubt, repentance, joy — require a human presence that can be wounded and still show up.

Your limitations are not weaknesses to overcome. They are part of what makes you trustworthy. Pastoral authority that comes through shared suffering cannot be replicated by a tool that has never suffered anything. AI has no scars. That is not a strength.
“
You cannot Google the answers to the deep questions of your life. No AI prompt will ever generate the purpose of your existence. You have to live it in the Atoms.
Dr. Arthur Brooks, Harvard — AI Advantage Summit 2026

The Hidden Cost of Automation

If no one gets trained
at the bottom, who
becomes senior?

The Stanford data shows entry-level workers aged 22-25 seeing the sharpest employment decline. This matters theologically as much as economically. Formation requires stages. You cannot skip them.

1
Senior lawyers are made by being junior lawyers first
The cases you lose at 25 form the wisdom you deploy at 45. There is no shortcut to that formation. When AI automates the entry level, it severs intergenerational knowledge transmission.
2
Ministry formation requires the same stages
The associate pastor making mistakes at 27 is being formed. The worship leader who struggles with a difficult congregation at 24 is being shaped. These stages are not inefficiencies to be removed. They are the thing itself.
3
The question for every leader in this room
How are you protecting the formation pathway for the people below you? What are you deliberately not automating because the struggle is the point?

The Prophetic Frame

In a synthetic world,
holiness is the
ultimate disruption.

As AI floods every platform with generated content and synthetic relationship, the things that cannot be generated become increasingly rare and — in every sense — precious.

Show up
in person, present, undistracted. In a synthetic world, physical presence is the premium product.
Tell truth
when it costs something. In a world of sycophantic AI, genuine honesty is the rarest currency.
Stay
when things get hard. In a world of frictionless exits, faithfulness through difficulty is extraordinary.
This is not a retreat into irrelevance. It is an advance into the thing only humans can offer. The question is whether the church recognises it in time to lead it.
