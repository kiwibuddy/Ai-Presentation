Dr. Arthur Brooks — Harvard — AI Advantage Summit 2026

Your brain is divided
for a reason.

Left Brain
Complicated Problems
How-to questions with definitive answers. Scheduling, data, logistics, route planning, drafting. These can be solved, closed and moved past. With enough processing power, a solution exists.
Give this to AI
Right Brain
Complex Problems
The deep mysteries of human experience. Love, grief, meaning, faith, relationships, vision, calling. These cannot be solved. They must be lived, navigated, and returned to again and again.
Humans only
Never use a human for a complicated task. Never use an AI for a complex one.
The trap is when we take a left-brain tool and try to use it to answer right-brain questions. AI will always disappoint when we ask it to do what only a human presence can do.

The Theology of Friction

AI is frictionless.
But humans grow
through friction.

AI never disagrees, never gets tired, never sins. It adapts to you instantly. There is no resistance. And that is precisely the problem.

What AI Offers
Frictionless Intimacy
A companion that always affirms, always adapts, never challenges, never walks away, never has a bad day of its own. Efficient. Available. Optimised for your engagement.
Simulation
What Humans Need
Genuine Resistance
Iron sharpens iron. Real formation happens when another person's reality pushes back against yours. Conflict, apology, forgiveness, difference — this is the texture of actual growth.
Formation
If we automate all the friction from our lives, we don't just become more efficient. We stop growing. The church's greatest irreplaceable offering is real, unoptimised, inconvenient human presence.

A broken human leader
is a more powerful vessel

The 90-Day Deployment Plan

Three steps.
Start Monday.

Not a full system overhaul. Not a tech investment. Three small decisions that, done consistently for 90 days, restructure how you spend your most valuable time.

1
Identify your Bit

Pick the single most time-consuming administrative task you do each week that doesn't require your personal presence or judgment. This week, find an AI workflow to handle it.

2
Build your Context Sandwich

Write a 200-word description of who you are, what you lead, and what "good" looks like in your work. Save it. Paste it at the start of every important AI interaction for the next 30 days.

3
Schedule the Atom time you just bought back

Before you do anything else: block the time in your calendar that your recovered hours go to. Name the person or relationship or practice it belongs to. Don't let efficiency fill it with more work.

4
Measure presence, not productivity

At 90 days, the question is not "how much more did I produce?" It's "how much more present was I?" That's the metric that tells you whether your deployment is actually working.
